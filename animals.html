Animal Collection

Discover more about the animals you can meet at the Jerusalem Jewish Zoo.

Zebra

Habitat: African grasslands

Diet: Grasses and herbs

Each zebra has a unique stripe pattern - like a barcode of identity.

Elephant

Habitat: Savanna & woodland

Diet: Leaves, bark, fruit

Elephants use their trunks to drink, greet friends, and grab snacks.

Meerkat

Habitat: Desert & open plains

Diet: Insects and small animals

While the group feeds, one meerkat always keeps watch for danger.

Red Panda

Habitat: Mountain forests

Diet: Bamboo, berries, eggs

Despite the name, red pandas are closer to raccoons than to giant pandas.

Kanguru

Habitat: Sahara, Desert, Australia

Diet: Grasses and herbs

Despite their large size, kangaroos can hop at speeds up to 35 miles per hour.

Cheetah

Habitat: African savannas

Diet: Small to medium-sized ungulates

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of reaching speeds up to 70 mph in short bursts.

Seal

Habitat: Ocean

Diet: Fish and squid

Seals can sleep underwater, surfacing for air without waking up.

Lion

Habitat: African savannas & grasslands

Diet: Large hoofed mammals like zebras and antelopes

Lions are the only big cats that live in social groups, called prides.

Giraffe

Habitat: African savannas & open woodlands

Diet: Leaves, shoots, and buds from tall trees

A giraffe's neck can be up to 2 meters long, yet it still has only seven neck vertebrae, the same as humans.

Penguin

Habitat: Rocky coasts and cold ocean waters

Diet: Fish, squid, and krill

Penguins are expert swimmers and can fly through the water at speeds over 20 km/h.

Orangutan

Habitat: Tropical rainforests of Borneo & Sumatra

Diet: Fruit, leaves, bark, and insects

Orangutans spend most of their lives in the treetops and build a fresh leafy nest to sleep in every night.

Flamingo

Habitat: Shallow lakes and lagoons

Diet: Algae, crustaceans, and small insects

Flamingos get their pink color from the carotenoid pigments in their diet.

Dolphin

Habitat: Coastal waters and open oceans

Diet: Fish, squid, and crustaceans

Dolphins use echolocation to navigate and hunt, sending out clicks and listening for the echoes.
